So you're here because you've heard about IPMAT and want to know what the fuss is about. Fair enough.

IPMAT stands for **Integrated Programme in Management Aptitude Test**. Fancy name, but here's the gist — it's the entrance exam for the 5-year Integrated Programme in Management (IPM) offered by IIM Indore (and a few other IIMs now). Think of it as a direct route from 12th grade to an MBA, skipping the whole graduation-then-MBA grind.

IIM Indore started the IPM program back in 2011, and it's been a solid option for anyone who knows early on that management is their thing. You finish school, spend 5 years at an IIM, and walk out with a Bachelor's + Master's degree. Not bad.

---

#### Which colleges accept IPMAT / JIPMAT?

- **IIM Indore** — The OG. Conducts IPMAT.
- **IIM Rohtak** — Conducts their own IPMAT (separate exam).
- **IIM Ranchi** — Accepts JIPMAT scores.
- **IIM Bodh Gaya** — Accepts JIPMAT scores.
- **Nirma University** — Has their own test but similar vibe.

JIPMAT is a joint exam conducted by NTA for IIM Ranchi and IIM Bodh Gaya. Yeah, the naming is confusing. Welcome to Indian entrance exams.

#### Eligibility Criteria

The short version: you need at least 60% in class 10th and 12th (55% for reserved categories) and should be born on or after a certain cutoff date (usually around August 1st, changes slightly each year).

Check the exact criteria on [IIM Indore's website](https://iimidr.ac.in/programmes/academic-programmes/five-year-integrated-programme-in-management-ipm/ipm-admissions-details/). Or use [this handy tool from AfterBoards](https://www.afterboards.in/ipmat-jipmat-iim-ipm-eligibility) to check if you're eligible in 2 minutes.

#### Aptitude Test Format

It's a Computer Based Test (CBT). Three sections, 90 questions total, 40 minutes each:

| Section | Questions | Type |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Quantitative Ability (SA) | 15 | Short Answer |
| Quantitative Ability (MCQ) | 30 | Multiple Choice |
| Verbal Ability | 45 | Multiple Choice |

Marking scheme: +4 for correct, -1 for incorrect. But here's a nice twist — **no negative marking for the Short Answer section**. So attempt all 15 QA-SA questions even if you're guessing.

---

#### Computation of Aptitude Test Score (ATS)

IIM Indore normalizes your score across all candidates. Here's the formula they use:

| Component | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| QA (MCQ) | $25 \times \left[ \frac{\text{Your Score} - \text{Min}}{\text{Max} - \text{Min}} \right]$ |
| QA (SA) | $25 \times \left[ \frac{\text{Your Score} - \text{Min}}{\text{Max} - \text{Min}} \right]$ |
| Verbal Ability (MCQ) | $50 \times \left[ \frac{\text{Your Score} - \text{Min}}{\text{Max} - \text{Min}} \right]$ |
| **ATS** | **Sum of the above** |

Basically, it's not just about raw marks — how you perform relative to everyone else matters too. Don't overthink this. Just focus on doing your best.

#### Personal Interview

~800 students get shortlisted for the PI round each year. The [cutoffs](https://www.afterboards.in/ipmat-jipmat-cutoff) vary.

The interview is a **veto round**. You can ace the written test but mess up the interview and it's over. Likewise, a great interview can make up for a slightly average score. Don't take it lightly.

There's a popular [guide from AfterBoards](https://lilcrows.notion.site/AfterBoards-Ultimate-Guide-to-IPM-Interviews-a9d8f713ad00422fa36035acdcf49801) that everyone reads. Read it. Then check out the transcripts section on this site for real interview experiences.

#### Composite Score Calculation

Here's something interesting — IIM Indore does **not** explicitly consider your 10th or 12th marks in the final score.

| Component | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Aptitude Test Score (ATS) | 65% |
| Personal Interview | 35% |

Your ATS carries more weight, but the interview is the tiebreaker. Both matter.
